Planning your first trip to Sri Lanka with just one week? You're in for an incredible adventure! While one week isn't enough to see everything, this carefully crafted 7-day itinerary will give you the perfect introduction to the Pearl of the Indian Ocean - covering stunning beaches, ancient culture, wildlife encounters, and scenic train rides.

This route is designed for travelers visiting during the peak season (December-March) when the west and south coasts offer the best weather.

## Day 1: Arrival in Colombo + Transfer to Sigiriya

**Travel:** Colombo Airport to Sigiriya (170 km, 4-5 hours)

### Morning:
- **Land at Bandaranaike International Airport**
- Meet your driver/pick up rental car
-Stop for breakfast en route
- **Tip:** Start driving by 10 AM to reach Sigiriya by 3 PM

### Afternoon:
- Arrive in Sigiriya area
- Check into hotel
- **Optional:** Quick visit to Dambulla Cave Temple (if time permits)
- Relax by pool

### Evening:
- Traditional Sri Lankan dinner
- Early night to prepare for tomorrow's climb

**Where to Stay:**
- Budget: Sigiriya Village Hotel ($30-50)
- Mid-Range: Hotel Sigiriya ($60-100)
- Luxury: Aliya Resort & Spa ($150+)

**Budget:** $100-150 (transport, accommodation, meals)

**Pro Tips:**
- Book airport pickup in advance
- Exchange some currency at airport
- Get local SIM card for navigation

## Day 2: Sigiriya Rock & Polonnaruwa

### Morning (6:30 AM - 11 AM):
**Sigiriya Rock Fortress**
- Start climb at 7 AM (avoid heat and crowds)
- Explore ancient frescoes and mirror wall
- Summit for panoramic views
- Duration: 2-3 hours
- **Cost:** $36 per person

### Afternoon (12 PM - 6 PM):
**Drive to Polonnaruwa** (68 km, 1.5 hours)
- Lunch in Sigiriya or Polonnaruwa
- Cycle tour of ancient city ruins
- Visit Royal Palace, Gal Vihara, Lotus Pond
- **Cost:** $25 entry + $3 bike rental

### Evening:
- Return to Sigiriya
- Sunset at Pidurangala Rock (optional)
- Dinner at hotel

**Budget:** $80-100 (entries, meals, guide optional)

**Pro Tips:**
- Bring lots of water for Sigiriya climb
- Hire bicycles in Polonnaruwa (much better than walking)
- Wear comfortable shoes

---

## Day 3: Minneriya Safari + Kandy

### Morning (6 AM - 11 AM):
**Minneriya/Kaudulla Safari**
- Early morning jeep safari
- Spot elephants, leopards, deer, birds
- Best during dry season (May-September for elephant gathering)
- Duration: 3-4 hours
- **Cost:** $40-60 per person

### Midday:
**Transfer to Kandy** (85 km, 2.5 hours)
- Scenic drive through hill country
- Lunch en route
- Check into Kandy hotel

### Afternoon/Evening:
**Kandy Exploration**
- Temple of the Tooth Relic ($10)
- Walk around Kandy Lake
- Botanical Gardens (optional, $10)
- Cultural dance show ($10, 5 PM)
- Explore Kandy city center

**Where to Stay:**
- Budget: Backpacker hostels ($15-25)
- Mid-Range: Swiss Residence ($40-70)
- Luxury: Earl's Regency ($100+)

**Budget:** $90-120

**Pro Tips:**
- Book safari the day before
- Modest dress for temple (cover shoulders/knees)
- Cultural show is worth it!

## Day 4: Kandy to Ella (Scenic Train Journey)

### Morning:
- Early breakfast
- **Kandy to Ella Train** (HIGHLIGHT!)
- Depart: 8:45 AM or 11:10 AM
- **Book tickets in advance!**
- Duration: 6-7 hours
- **Cost:** $8-15 (2nd/3rd class)

**This is one of the world's most scenic train journeys!**

### What to Expect on the Train:
- Tea plantations as far as the eye can see
- Nine Arches Bridge crossing
- Mist-covered mountains
- Waterfalls
- Local villages
- **Hang out of open doors for photos** (be safe!)

### Afternoon/Evening:
- Arrive in Ella
- Check into hotel
- Short town walk
- Sunset from your guesthouse
- Dinner at Caf√© Chill or Matey Hut

**Where to Stay:**
- Budget: Ella Flower Garden/Dream Caf√© ($20-35)
- Mid-Range: 98 Acres ($50-80)
- Luxury: Ambiente Hotel ($100+)

**Budget:** $60-80

**Pro Tips:**

## Day 6: Ella to Yala to Mirissa

### Morning:
- Check out early (7 AM)
- **Transfer to Yala** (100 km, 2.5 hours)

### Midday (10 AM - 2 PM):
**Yala National Park Safari**
- Best national park for leopard sightings!
- Also see elephants, sloth bears, crocodiles
- Duration: 3-4 hours
- **Cost:** $50-75 per person
- **Book in advance!**

### Afternoon:
- Lunch near Yala
- **Transfer to Mirissa** (140 km, 3 hours)
- Check into beach hotel
- Beach time and sunset
- Fresh seafood dinner

**Where to Stay:**
- Budget: Sweet Luppie/Zephyr ($25-40)
- Mid-Range: Mi Amor Vila ($60-90)
- Luxury: Cape Weligama ($200+)

**Budget:** $100-130

**Pro Tips:**
- Early morning safaris are best
- Bring sunscreen and hat
- Book beach hotel with pool for luxury

## Day 7: Mirissa Relaxation + Return

### Morning:
**Choose One:**

**Option A: Whale Watching** (Dec-Apr season)
- Start 6:30 AM
- See blue whales and dolphins
- Duration: 3-4 hours
- **Cost:** $35-50
- **Book day before**

**Option B: Beach Day**
- Sleep in
- Relax on Mirissa Beach
- Snorkeling
- Surf lessons ($20-30)

### Midday:
- Beach lunch at Zephyr or Dewmini
- Final swim

### Afternoon:
- **Quick stop in Galle** (40 km, 1 hour)
- Walk Galle Fort (1-2 hours)
- Shopping for souvenirs
- Photo opportunities

### Evening:
- **Transfer to Colombo** (120 km, 2.5 hours)
- Dinner in Colombo or at airport
- Depart or overnight depending on flight

**Budget:** $80-120

**Pro Tips:**
- Book whale watching in advance
- Galle Fort is UNESCO site - don't skip!
- Allow 3 hours airport buffer
- Buy tea/spices in Galle

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Is 7 days enough for Sri Lanka?**  
A: For highlights, yes! For everything, no. This route covers must-sees. Plan 10-14 days for a more relaxed pace.

**Q: Can I do this itinerary with kids?**  
A: Yes! Skip harder hikes, addmore beach time, choose family-friendly hotels.

**Q: What if my flight lands in the evening?**  
A: Stay in Colombo or near airport first night, start fresh next morning.

**Q: Can I reverse this route?**  
A: Yes! Start in Galle/Mirissa and end in Sigiriya. Train goes both directions.

**Q: is it safe?**  
A: Sri Lanka is very safe for tourists. Take normal precautions.

**Q: Do I need a visa?**  
A: Most nationals need ETA (electronic visa). Apply online before travel ($50).
